FAQ

Frequently asked questions about Lunar Forge 1.0

Lunar Forge 1.0 is an 18-hour hackathon. Hacking begins at 11:00 AM on March 6 and submissions close at 5:00 AM on March 7, 2026.

Yes! You can participate solo, as a duo (2 members), or as a trio (3 members). Choose the team size that works best for you.

You need to submit a working prototype with a GitHub repository link, a project description, tech stack used, and optionally a demo video and presentation slides.

Projects are judged on: Innovation (30%), Technical Complexity (25%), Design & UX (20%), Impact & Feasibility (15%), and Presentation (10%).

Problem statement changes are allowed until the hacking phase begins. Once hacking starts at 11:00 AM on March 6, your selected problem statement is locked.

No restrictions! You can use any programming language, framework, API, or tool. AI-assisted coding tools (Copilot, ChatGPT, etc.) are allowed but must be disclosed in your submission.

Late submissions will not be accepted. Make sure to submit your project before 5:00 AM on March 7. We recommend submitting early and updating if needed.

Reach out to the organizers via the WhatsApp channel. Mentors will also be available during designated mentor sessions for 1-on-1 guidance.
